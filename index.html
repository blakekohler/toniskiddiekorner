About


The Preschool Place, formerly known as Kiddie Korner Preschool and Toni’s Preschool Place, was founded in September of 2013 when Toni decided to pursue her dream of starting a preschool in her basement. Although she started alone with just a couple of children, her preschool philosophy and business quickly started to grow. In September of 2015, Toni hired Megan Zeitner and it was the best decision she could have made for her business! Megan’s natural talent, love of teaching young children, and her belief in play-based learning was the perfect compliment to Toni’s preschool curriculum and philosophy. Together, Toni and Megan have decided to re-brand into The Preschool Place. They believe deeply in the power of learning through play-based experiences and they want to give this gift to as many children as they can, which is why they are always striving to add more locations.

Meet Toni

I began teaching out of my home in September of 2013. I graduated from Utah State University with a dual bachelor’s degree in Elementary Education and Early Childhood Development. I also have an endorsement in teaching English as a second language. After graduating, I got a job teaching Kindergarten in Alpine School District and taught for three years. I also taught English online to children in China. I love young children and the magic that learning can bring into their lives.

As a former kindergarten teacher, I have seen the curriculum become increasingly demanding. The bar has been raised and benchmarks have been heightened. While I know what is expected of children at the kindergarten level, I also know that research has shown that children who participate in play and discovery-based learning in their early childhood are more successful in later life. I want to prepare your child to meet and exceed kindergarten expectations according to what is developmentally appropriate to them. I believe that children learn best through play and real-life experiences. I take a constructivist approach and use play to motivate and plan curriculum and learning.

I know that this is a critical time in your child’s life. That is why I teach to the whole child, to cover all of the areas of their unique development.

I truly believe that I have a once in a lifetime opportunity to create a love for learning in children’s hearts. I want to inspire and instill in your child a deep love for imagining, creating, playing, and learning. They will develop a strong learning foundation to last them for the rest of their lives!

Our Preschool Setup

Our preschool offers a special learning environment for our children. Because we believe in play-based learning, we base our classroom structure off of the nationally accredited Adele and Dale Young Child Development Laboratory at Utah State University. Our classrooms offer active exploration, promoting independence, creative thinking, problem-solving strategies, curiosity, self-help skills, and interpersonal skills.

All of our classrooms include the following (Click on each to learn more):

  • Art & Art Easel center

    Art & Art Easel center


    We believe in process art! When doing art projects, we focus on the process of the art, rather than the finished product. Although our students might not go home with a finished “Pinterest worthy” art project, they will leave with increased confidence because they were encouraged to create according to their own interests. We encourage our students to find joy in creating using multiple art mediums without the added pressure of having to produce a modeled product.
  • Writing center

    Writing center


    Our writing and art center encourages children to explore writing letters using different writing mediums. We make sure to switch out and add different materials to keep students engaged and curious.
  • Reading center

    Reading center


    We love to take a deep dive into books and stories! Our students develop their literacy skills in a number of ways. They listen to stories during large group time. They reflect on those stories in small groups during our unit study. And they have the opportunity to reinvestigate stories, characters and storylines during playtime at the reading center.
  • Dramatic play

    Dramatic play


    Dramatic Play, or pretend play is a key component in our classroom! Children use dramatic play to explore feelings, expand imagination, gain social skills, develop literacy skills, and learn to self-regulate. Our Dramatic Play area changes themes with each unit of study.
  • Sensory table

    Sensory table


    Our sensory table offers a great way for children to explore their senses and the world around them. We’ve found that sensory play encourages the development of small motor skills, supports language development, encourages ‘scientific thinking’ and problem solving, and builds nerve connections in the brain. Because the items in our sensory table are switched out often, our students are always actively exploring and engaging.
  • Large motor block and play

    Large motor block and play


    We know how important it is for children to exercise and strengthen their gross muscles, which is why we include a large motor play area in our preschool classrooms. Each preschool location offers a Jungle Jumparoo. This allows them to jump, hang, swing, and climb while also using their imagination. We also regularly bring out items such as a balance beam, basketball hoop, hula hoops, etc. for the children to play with.
  • Small motor blocks and manipulatives

    Small motor blocks and manipulatives


    We offer many different blocks and manipulatives for the children to play with. These get switched out according to student interests. There are many benefits to block building and playing with small manipulatives. These include small/fine motor development, turn-taking and social skills, hand-eye coordination, math skills, and engineering skills. It’s also a great way to build vocabulary.
  • Music and Movement

    Music and Movement


    We incorporate music and movement into every day! We love to add musical instruments, scarves, costumes, and props to offer variety as we sing, dance, and move our bodies to the beat!
  • Cooking experiences

    Cooking experiences


    We love to engage our students in cooking experiences! Within every thematic unit of study, we incorporate a cooking experience. We involve the children in every step of the process. They get to help read recipes, gather ingredients, measure, mix, scoop, and EAT, of course!
  • Outside play (according to weather)

    Outside play (according to weather)


    We offer outside play as much as possible! Unless the weather is too cold or uncomfortable for the children, we go outside, even in the snow! The children love to play and build in the sand box, swing on the swings, and ride bikes or scooters. We are always adding new toys and activities for outside play to keep our curious students engaged and busy!

We believe in teaching to the whole child and their many different learning abilities and interests. Within our classroom walls, your child will find safety and comfort in exploring each learning area. Each area will change every couple of weeks according to our thematic learning units, so your child will always find something new and interesting to keep his or her busy mind engaged while problem solving, creating, imagining, and most importantly, LEARNING!

  • 1 hour of play (including outside play if weather permits)
  • Small Group time
  • Large Group time
  • Snack time
  • Individual one-on-one time with a teacher to work on learning concepts.

Multiple field trips will be offered throughout the year. Field trips will include small, local trips (such as a walk to the park, a trip to the fire station, a picnic, a letter walk) and a couple of major field trips where parents and transportation will be needed.

For our major field trips, parents will be expected to meet us at the field trip with their child, or work out an arrangement for a ride for their child. Teachers may be able to take children to field trips if space in their vehicle is available.

Snack time is the best time to teach manners and social skills! Your child will be offered a snack every day that they come to school. Snack will include a small food item and a drink (juice, milk, water). We like to offer healthy snacks as a way of introducing new foods and promoting healthy habits. With the exception of cooking experiences, we try to stay away from high-sugar snacks. Most snacks will be incorporated into the theme and will be mix-matched to make snack time fun and exciting.

Food Allergy

We understand and are sensitive to food allergies that your child may have. We will cater to your child’s allergies and make sure that they will still be able to participate in the fun! If your child has a severe allergy, we may ask you to bring products for us to have on hand for your child.

We align our curriculum with the Utah State Core Curriculum standards. We believe in play! All of the concepts will be taught in a fun and engaging way. Because we believe in play-based learning, we do not believe in pushing children to read until they are ready. We follow the Fountas & Pinnell’s phonics program. This program introduces letters, literacy, and phonics. We will teach beginning reading if your child is interested and ready. Book bags will be sent home each week to give your child some exciting new choices when reading each night. Homework will be provided, but is not required. With a curriculum based around play, your child will learn all of the following (including concepts from the 3-year-old class):

With a curriculum based around play, your child will learn all of the following (including concepts from the 3 year old class):

  • Counting to 30
  • Basic number Sense skills (one-one correspondence, representing last number shown, basic adding and subtracting to 5)
  • Sorting/categorizing
  • Alphabet Letters
  • Alphabet Sounds
  • Correct Writing formations for each letter (This will be reinforced daily- Very important!
  • Rhyming
  • Blending
  • Segmenting
  • Syllables
  • Beginning sight words (high frequency words)
  • Early Literacy Concepts
  • Phonological Awareness
  • Letter/Sound Awareness

 

Consider this preschool class to be an intermediate class, or in-between the Little Learners Class and the Kinder-Prep class. It will follow the same curriculum as the kinder-prep class, but at a slower rate, since it is only two days per week. We have combined the ages to give siblings the opportunity to attend preschool together, and to also give more options for pricing and schedule. If your child will have just turned three years old by the time preschool starts, we strongly suggest that you enroll them in the little Learners Class. If your child is on the older side of three (will be turning four shortly after the school year begins, this class would be a better fit for them.) We will split the class into groups during learning time to give the best support for your child.

This class will be the perfect starting point for your child! Book bags will be sent home each week to give your child some exciting new choices when reading each night.

While discovering the world around them, your child will learn:

  • Shapes
  • Colors
  • Recognizing Alphabet letters
  • Recognizing Alphabet Sounds
  • Sorting/categorizing
  • Counting to 10
  • Basic number sense foundations
  • Science concepts- (Taught through the weekly theme)
